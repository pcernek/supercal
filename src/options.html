Supercal Options

Setting up Google Calendar API

1

Go to the Google Cloud Console and create a new project.

2

Enable the Google Calendar API for your project:

  1. In the sidebar, click on "APIs & Services" > "Library"
  2. Search for "Google Calendar API"
  3. Click on the API and then click "Enable"
3

Create OAuth credentials:

  1. In the sidebar, click on "APIs & Services" > "Credentials"
  2. Click "Create Credentials" > "OAuth client ID"
  3. Select "Chrome Extension" as the application type
  4. Enter a name for your OAuth client (e.g., "Supercal Extension")
  5. For "Application ID", enter your extension ID. You can find this in Chrome by going to chrome://extensions, enabling Developer Mode, and looking at the ID for Supercal.
4

Copy your Client ID and update your manifest.json file:

"oauth2": {
  "client_id": "YOUR_CLIENT_ID_HERE",
  "scopes": [
    "https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar.readonly"
  ]
}

Note: After updating your manifest.json, you'll need to reload the extension in Chrome.

Using the Extension

Once you've set up the Google Calendar API:

  1. Click on the Supercal icon in your browser toolbar
  2. Click "Sign In" to authenticate with Google
  3. Select a date range and click "Fetch Calendar Data"
  4. The extension will fetch your calendar events and display time totals on the Google Calendar page

Privacy

Supercal only accesses your Google Calendar data with your permission. Your data is processed locally in your browser and is not sent to any external servers.

The extension only requests read-only access to your calendar data.
